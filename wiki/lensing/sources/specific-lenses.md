# Sources: specific lenses and oddities

## Tuntsov et al. 2016 — dynamic spectral mapping

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Reynolds2015`
**Reference:** ApJ 817, 176; arXiv:1512.03411; doi:10.3847/0004-637X/817/2/176
**Concepts:** [[plasma-lensing]] (forward-ref)

**Supports:**
- A dynamic radio spectrum can be mapped to a plasma lens's electron column-density profile under axisymmetric or fully anisotropic assumptions.
- Applying the method to the PKS 1939-315 extreme scattering event gives similar profiles for both assumed geometries at 4.2–10.8 GHz.
- Residuals and lower-frequency discrepancies show that more sophisticated inversion models are required.

**Use when:**
- Citing dynamic-spectrum inversion of an observed interstellar plasma-lensing event.

**Do not use for:**
- Strong gravitational lensing or a geometry-independent plasma-lens reconstruction.

## Young, Keeton & Baker 2022 — resolved-kinematics reconstruction

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Young2022`
**Reference:** ApJ 929, 6; arXiv:2202.13921; doi:10.3847/1538-4357/ac59af
**Concepts:** [[source-reconstruction]], [[regularization]], [[kinematics-and-lensing]]

**Supports:**
- Three-dimensional regularization uses resolved kinematic information when reconstructing lensed integral-field data cubes.
- Mock ALMA tests recover intensity and velocity structure more accurately than independent two-dimensional channel reconstructions.
- The 3D approach gives more uniform noise and resolution and is less sensitive to individual-channel signal-to-noise.

**Use when:**
- Citing pixelized reconstruction methods for strongly lensed galaxies with resolved spectral kinematics.

**Do not use for:**
- Generic multiband colour regularization or validation on observed ALMA lenses.

## Shajib et al. 2022 — WGD 2038-4008 software comparison

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Shajib2022`
**Reference:** A&A 667, A123; arXiv:2202.11101; doi:10.1051/0004-6361/202243401
**Concepts:** [[time-delay-cosmography]], [[lens-modeling-systematics]] (forward-ref)

**Supports:**
- Independent blind GLEE and lenstronomy models give time-delay predictions consistent within 1.2 sigma after incorporating stellar kinematics and external convergence.
- Different reconstructed PSFs explain a substantial discrepancy in power-law slope and external shear between the initial models.
- Using the same PSF reconstruction brings the two modelling results into close agreement.

**Use when:**
- Citing cross-code lens-modelling comparisons or PSF-reconstruction systematics in time-delay cosmography.

**Do not use for:**
- A strong-lens catalogue, a measured time delay, or a final Hubble-constant inference for WGD 2038-4008.
