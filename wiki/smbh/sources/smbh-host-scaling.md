## Gebhardt 2000 — M–σ relation

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Gebhardt2000`
**Reference:** A Relationship between Nuclear Black Hole Mass and Galaxy Velocity Dispersion; doi:10.1086/312840; ApJ
**Concepts:** [[smbh-host-scaling]]

**Supports:**
- Reports an empirical relation between nuclear black-hole mass and host-galaxy stellar velocity dispersion.
- Provides one of the foundational observational M–σ relation references.
- Supports using velocity dispersion as a host-galaxy correlate of SMBH mass.

**Use when:**
- Citing the early observational M–σ relation.

**Do not use for:**
- Later recalibrations, Sérsic-index correlations, or total-stellar-mass relations.

**Concepts:** [[smbh-host-scaling]]

**Supports:**
- Reports two black holes with masses near ten billion solar masses in giant elliptical galaxies.
- Extends the high-mass end of empirical SMBH-host scaling samples.
- Motivates caution about the upper tail and intrinsic scatter of SMBH scaling relations.

**Use when:**
