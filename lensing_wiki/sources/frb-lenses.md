# Sources: FRB and plasma lensing

Papers on the lensing of fast radio bursts (FRBs) and of pulsars by
plasma structures along the line of sight — a frequency-dependent
relative of gravitational lensing.

## Er & Rogers 2019 — elliptical plasma lenses

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Er2019`
**Reference:** MNRAS 488, 5651–5664; arXiv:1907.10787; doi:10.1093/mnras/stz2073
**Concepts:** [[plasma-lensing]] (forward-ref)

**Supports:**
- The paper derives elliptical softened-power-law and exponential plasma-lens models.
- Ellipticity can increase the complexity of critical curves and magnification maps.
- Plasma lensing is wavelength dependent and can strongly demagnify background sources.

**Use when:**
- Citing non-axisymmetric plasma-lens models or ellipticity-dependent lensing structure.

**Do not use for:**
- Evidence for two observed populations of plasma lenses.

## Er, Yang & Rogers 2020 — dispersion-measure bias

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Er2020`
**Reference:** arXiv:2001.02100; doi:10.3847/1538-4357/ab66b1
**Concepts:** [[plasma-lensing]]

**Supports:**
- Plasma-lensing delays contain both geometric and dispersive contributions.
- The geometric delay can be comparable to the dispersive delay for dense plasma gradients.
- Ignoring refraction can substantially bias a fast radio burst's inferred dispersion measure.

**Use when:**
- Discussing plasma-lensing systematics in dispersion measures inferred from FRB arrival times.

**Do not use for:**
- A measurement of the plasma content or host contribution for a specific observed FRB.

## Rogers & Er 2019 — dual-component plasma lenses

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Rogers2019`
**Reference:** MNRAS 485, 5800–5816; arXiv:1903.06384; doi:10.1093/mnras/stz744
**Concepts:** [[plasma-lensing]]

**Supports:**
- Dual-component exponential and softened-power-law plasma lenses produce complex critical and caustic structures.
- Their light curves can be more complicated than those of isolated plasma lenses.
- Monitoring an extreme scattering event can help distinguish otherwise degenerate models.

**Use when:**
- Citing multiple-component plasma-lens morphology, light curves, or model degeneracy.

**Do not use for:**
- Claims about dust composition or an empirical detection of a dual-component plasma lens.
