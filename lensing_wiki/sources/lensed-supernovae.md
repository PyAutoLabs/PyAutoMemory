# Sources: lensed supernovae

## Goobar et al. 2017 — iPTF16geu

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Goobar2016`
**Reference:** Science 356, 291–295; arXiv:1611.00014; doi:10.1126/science.aal2729
**Concepts:** [[lensed-supernovae]]

**Supports:**
- iPTF16geu is a multiply imaged Type Ia supernova at redshift 0.409.
- High-resolution observations resolve four images around the foreground lensing galaxy.
- The total magnification exceeds 50, while the relative image magnifications indicate lens-galaxy substructure.

**Use when:**
- Citing the discovery and resolved lensing configuration of iPTF16geu.

**Do not use for:**
- Time-delay cosmography or a population forecast for lensed supernovae.

## Rydberg et al. 2020 — high-redshift lensed supernova forecasts

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Rydberg2018`
**Reference:** MNRAS 491, 2447–2459; arXiv:1805.02662; doi:10.1093/mnras/stz3203
**Concepts:** [[lensed-supernovae]]

**Supports:**
- Gravitational magnification can bring supernovae at redshift 5–7 within LSST detection limits.
- The LSST wide survey could detect up to 120 lensed Population I and II supernovae under the paper's assumptions.
- Deeper survey strategies could detect core-collapse and pair-instability supernovae beyond the wide-survey limit.

**Use when:**
- Citing LSST forecasts for strongly lensed supernovae at redshift 5–7.

**Do not use for:**
- An observed lensed-supernova sample or forecasts for Roman or JWST.
