# Sources: WDM and Lyman-alpha

Papers on warm-dark-matter constraints from the Lyman-α forest — the
complementary cosmological probe to strong-lensing substructure
constraints on WDM thermal-relic masses.

## Viel et al. 2013 — high-redshift Lyman-alpha constraints

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Viel2013`
**Reference:** Phys. Rev. D 88, 043502; arXiv:1306.2314; doi:10.1103/PhysRevD.88.043502
**Concepts:** [[dark-matter-physics]]

**Supports:**
- High-resolution spectra of 25 quasars above redshift 4 constrain thermal-relic WDM through the Lyman-alpha flux power spectrum.
- Hydrodynamical simulations and conservative systematic errors give a two-sigma lower limit of 3.3 keV on the thermal-relic mass.
- The resulting free-streaming bound leaves little room for WDM to resolve the small-scale CDM crisis under the paper's assumptions.

**Use when:**
- Citing the 2013 HIRES/MIKE Lyman-alpha constraint on thermal-relic WDM.

**Do not use for:**
- Sterile-neutrino limits or a model-independent bound on every non-cold dark-matter scenario.

## Iršič et al. 2017 — combined Lyman-alpha constraints

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Irsic2017`
**Reference:** Phys. Rev. D 96, 023522; arXiv:1702.01764; doi:10.1103/PhysRevD.96.023522
**Concepts:** [[dark-matter-physics]]

**Supports:**
- The analysis combines intermediate-redshift XQ-100 and high-redshift HIRES/MIKE Lyman-alpha flux-power measurements.
- Under a smooth power-law thermal history, the combined data give a two-sigma thermal-relic WDM lower limit of 5.3 keV.
- Allowing abrupt intergalactic-medium temperature evolution weakens the combined lower limit to 3.5 keV.

**Use when:**
- Citing how combined Lyman-alpha data and thermal-history assumptions affect WDM limits.

**Do not use for:**
- A thermal-history-independent 5.3 keV bound.

## Bonaca et al. 2019 — GD-1 spur and gap

**Canonical BibTeX key:** `Bonaca2019`
**Reference:** ApJ 880, 38; arXiv:1811.03631; doi:10.3847/1538-4357/ab2873
**Concepts:** [[dark-matter-substructure]]

**Supports:**
- A fast perturber encounter can jointly reproduce the observed GD-1 stream gap and off-stream spur.
- Preferred perturber models span roughly one million to one hundred million solar masses.
- Known globular clusters, dwarf galaxies, and molecular clouds are disfavoured, making dark substructure the most plausible explanation considered.

**Use when:**
- Citing stellar-stream morphology as evidence for a compact Milky Way halo perturber.

**Do not use for:**
- A Lyman-alpha WDM bound or definitive identification of the perturber as a dark-matter subhalo.
